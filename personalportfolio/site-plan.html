Content

Home page - Types of Activities Visitors Will Complete and Necessary Information for Visitors to Complete Actions

[On my website, visitors will primarily be interested in viewing my portfolio and learning about my skills. They may also want to download my resume or connect with me for possible projects or job opportunities. The activities they'll likely complete on my site could include browsing through my portfolio to review specific case studies, projects, or creative works I have done. I will also provide a contact form or links to my social media accounts to make it easy for visitors to reach out to me. Some may want to engage in a conversation, asking questions about my work or requesting collaboration opportunities. To complete any action on my site, such as reaching out or hiring me, visitors will need clear, easy-to-find contact details. This could be an email address, a contact form, or links to my social media profiles. If a visitor is looking to hire me, providing details about my availability and the type of work I am open to will be essential. If they are exploring my work, they should have access to thorough descriptions of my past projects, including challenges faced, solutions implemented, and final outcomes. Additionally, I will provide my resume in a downloadable format so that visitors can quickly see a summary of my qualifications. This should include details such as my work experience, education, skills, and certifications. Some visitors might want to access my portfolio in a more detailed or downloadable format as well. ]

[Page 2 - Why My Visitors Will Choose My Website]

[I believe visitors will choose my website over others because it will offer a personal, engaging experience that emphasizes quality over quantity. By offering clear, well-organized sections and an easy-to-navigate design, visitors will be able to quickly find what they're looking for. Ultimately, my website will stand out because it tells my story. I will show where I have worked in the past. It highlights not only my skills but my passion, commitment, and the quality of my work. By fostering a personal connection with visitors, my website will become a space where potential clients or employers feel confident in reaching out to me for collaboration. The combination of strong design, personalized content, and clear calls to action will make my portfolio website the go-to resource for learning more about what I offer. ]

Images for the Page 2

[Page 3 - Why people will choose my page]

[People will visit my portfolio website to learn more about my work, skills, and the projects I've completed. As a personal portfolio, the goal is to showcase my abilities and demonstrate how my work aligns with the needs of potential employers, clients, or collaborators. Visitors will come seeking a comprehensive, visually appealing display of my accomplishments and a clear representation of my professional abilities. I want them to get a sense of who I am, how I approach problems, and what kind of projects I’m capable of handling. Whether they're looking for a designer, developer, or writer, my website will offer a glimpse into my versatility and adaptability in various fields. Questions Visitors May Have One of the key questions visitors will expect my site to answer is: "What can this person do for me?" They want to know what value I can bring to their projects or company. They might also want to know about my background—such as education, experience, and any specific expertise in certain areas. Visitors will also likely ask, “What have they worked on?” and expect to see a portfolio of work that demonstrates the quality and breadth of my capabilities. They may also inquire about my availability or how to get in touch with me, so clear contact information and call-to-action buttons will be crucial.
